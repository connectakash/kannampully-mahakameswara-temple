About the Celebration

Nataraja Natanolsavam is a seven-day celebration conducted every year from December 23 to 29 as part of the ritual offerings to Lord Mahakameswara. The festival is dedicated to Lord Nataraja, the cosmic dancer whose tandava symbolizes creation, preservation, and dissolution. During this sacred period, the temple complex resonates with the sound of bells, mridangam, veena, and Vedic chants, preparing the space for classical dancers to offer their art.

Artists performing classical Indian dance forms‚ÄîBharatanatyam, Mohiniyattam, Kuchipudi, Kathak, Odissi, Manipuri, Sattriya, and allied traditions‚Äîare invited to participate. Each dancer treats the sacred stage as the Chidambaram of the heart, surrendering their movements as a ritual offering. The curated presentations explore themes such as Ananda Tandava, Panchabhuta (five elements), and the cosmic wheel of time.

Nataraja Natanolsavam is not just a cultural program; it is a living continuity of temple art traditions. Families and devotees gather every evening to witness divinity expressed through rhythm, mudras, and bhava. The atmosphere culminates in a final day homage, with lamps, flowers, and collective chants seeking the blessings of Mahakameswara.
